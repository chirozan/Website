About

Born in Austria, I studied at the University of Applied Arts in Vienna, where I completed my Magister of Art in 2015. In 2017, I decided to move to Bulgaria, where I experiment with the topics of art and nature. After several years of exploration, I fully dedicated myself to visual art, especially drawing and painting. These traditional techniques allow me to interact with my surroundings and work independently, relying only on myself and the weather.

I mainly work outdoors because, as a visual artist, I examine the complex relationship between humans and nature. My work focuses on how we shape, control, and manipulate our environment. In my creative process, I question the landscapes, animals, and plants that survive in curated spaces.

"What do I see when I look out the window, stroll through a park, visit the countryside, or relax in a garden?" The manicured lawns, backyard apple trees, and city park rosebushes exist because we put them there. Our obsession with beauty and order defines how we see the world. I don’t know wild nature - only cultivated land. My work reflects this paradox: we destroy to create, and call the result 'natural.'
